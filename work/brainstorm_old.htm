The Legend of Zelda: Book of Mudora - Brainstorming Document

  • Goal: To stop the Dark Tribe from destroying Hyrule with civil war by resolving racial conflicts, obtaining the entirety of the Book of Mudora and finding and defeating the Tribe.
  • Setting: Hyrule (both South and North lands, over all provinces) on the Child Timeline a few generations after Twilight Princess (but before FSA).

  • Subgoals:
    • Explore temples and defeat temple bosses in order to gain powerful items to assist you in your goal.
    • Explore areas and assist different races in their conflicts.

  • Dark Tribe Members:
    • Current:
      • Carock (Wizard)
      • Zirna (Enchantress)
      • Dark Link(s)
      • Belahim (Leader)
    • Historical:
      • Ghirahim
      • Zant, Midna
      • the Twili people
      • Agahnim
      • Veran
      • the Zuna people
      • Malladus
      • Majora

I, Mudora, have been appointed as Sage and Royal Historian to our great King. His Majesty has tasked me with the documentation our young world's life, one that will be free of error and last unto the end of time. So that our descendants may recall the early days of the world's history and have the knowledge of the beginning from those who witness it, it is my wish to create a record that can be passed down from generation to generation; one that will record every detail of our new world's life, from beginning to end. This shall be my contribution to the future greatness of Hyrule...

PROGRESSION

  • Ordon Village
    • Ordon Festival is underway! Talk to villagers (including Mayor Ulo who tells you to get his lantern out of his house), then race to win the Ordon Shield from the Blacksmith. Speak with Spirit Ordona (in a torch), then Julita who directs you to the sewers to find Crista. When she tells you about the sewer access, check it out to find the Ordon Sword then save her from ChuChus who appear afterward.
      • Shop (Shield, replacement costs 100 rupees. Bomb bag, can't get until after 2nd wallet. Potions included as well)
  • Eastern Ruins/Hyrulian Sewers (Ongoing Temple) - Earth/Shadow/Water themes
  • Ordon Village
    • After getting shield and sword, head back to Ordon Village and talk to Ordona who directs you to Faron Woods to find the Sacred Grove. Near the grove the Deku and Tokay are having a conflict over who can have the area, with the Tokay having recently arrived and the Deku not liking it. Monkeys are also there, watching the drama unfold.
  • Sacred Grove - Sacred Grove Temple (2nd) - Forest/Earth themes
  • Upon leaving the temple, the monkeys steal your Book of Mudora page and run off through the woods to the beach.
  • Beach
    • Gerudo are there with their crashed ship, blocking access to the beach which they're claiming. They ask you to find parts to repair their ship (you not knowing they're turning it into an airship) and agree to leave the beach when you do. They tell you about the Abandoned Pyramid in the desert which should contain the parts they need. They can't get in because it requires a special power (chosen by the Triforce) and they see that you have it.
  • Desert Expanse - Abandoned Pyramid (3rd) - Desert/Metal/Shadow themes (remnants of Lanayru Mining Facility)
  • Beach
    • Give the parts to the Gerudo and they leave the beach for the desert (after informing Link of their true intentions). Return to the woods to tell the Tokay the beach is theirs, thereby resolving the turf war.
  • Faron Woods
    • The monkeys are bored now that the Deku/Tokay conflict is over, so they turn their attention to Link. They jump up into the trees, throwing the page back and forth between them. Link has to shake a tree to get a monkey out, and has to pick the right one with the page.
    • Dekus see the monkeys give the Book page back to Link and mention the existance of the Library and suggest how to access it from the sewers/ruins.
  • Kasuto Town - Ancient Library
    • Isan (Kasuto's page) tells you that Kasuto was killed by a group of strange people who were trying to use the Book of Mudora to cast a spell. He tells you about how Kasuto divided the Book of Mudora before he died and about the Library. Read what books you can in the library, more will be readable as you find more of the Book of Mudora. After some reading, talk to Isan again to receive a copy of the Hyrulean Bestiary to give to the Relic Collector in Castle Town in exchange for the Ocarina of Wind)

    RACES

    • Hylians (Major humanoid race of Hyrule, almost elf-like)
      • Culture based on Medieval (Arthurian) England
      • Capital is Hyrule Castle Town in the center of Hyrule
      • Represent Politics (home of the Council of Elders), element of Spirit
      • Major City is Kakariko City in the northwest of Hyrule, represent Culture (Riverside Ampitheater), element of Shadow
      • Elder: Princess Zelda (female, Princess of Hyrule, "7th Sage, Sage of Time")
      • Major Characters: Gaira, Kasuto, Isan, Rowin, Moriss, Gartan, Etnaya, Strap, Garroth, Astra
    • Ordonians/Humans (Humans who mostly seclude themselves in Ordon Forest)
      • Culture based on Celtic and other pre-English European cultures
      • Capital is Ordon Village in the south of Hyrule
      • Represent Agriculture (selling Pumpkins, Goats, etc.), element of Light
      • Major city is Saria in North Hyrule, element of Forest
      • Elder: Mayor Ulo (male, also Mayor of Ordon Village, "Sage of Light")
    • Major Characters: Julita, Crista, Tristan, Francis/Quint/Jarred, Tern, Turt
    • Dekus (Bush people who live only in the Lost Woods)
      • Culture based on African (sub-Saharan) cultures
    • Tokay (Lizard people who live on the Beach. Avid traders)
      • Culture based on Carribean/Native American cultures
      • Capital is the tent city they've set up on the beach
      • Represent Trade (don't deal in currency, only trade items), element of Forest
      • Elder: Tokage Gin (female, "Sage of Forest")
    • Gerudo (Mysterious woman-only race who live in the Desert)
      • Culture based on Amazonian women, with an ancient Arabian (Persian) flair
      • Major city is Nabooru in North Hyrule, element of Spirit
      • Represent Transportation (build airships to pirate the skies, build tunnels into mountains to steal), element of Wind
      • Major Characters: Hesla
    • Rito (Flying race who live in Septen Heights, evolved from the Zora and maintain an amicable relationship)
      • Cutlture based on Incan (and other South American or sub-Mexican ancient) cultures
      • Capital is Septen Heights, a new rope city founded in the north of Hyrule
      • Represent Environment (power their cities with wind and water turbines), element of Spirit
      • Elder: Chieftain Koshi (male, relatively young, "Sage of Spirit")
    • Zora (Fish-like people who make their home at the mouth of a river, Zora's Domain)
      • Culture based on the Roman Empire
      • Represent Military (Isolationist, Defensive, Aggressive), element of Water
      • Elder: Elder Juba (female, advisor to King Zora, "Sage of Water")
    • Zola (Fish-like, sea-dwelling cousins of the Zoras who reside only in Eastern Hyrule)
      • Culture based on Greek culture
      • Capital is Ruto, the coast city in the North, element of Water
    • Gorons (Rock-like tride who reside in Death Mountain and have become industrialized. Avid miners)
      • Culture based on aboriginal Polynesian/Australian tribes
      • Capital is Goron City at the height of Death Mountain
      • Represent Industry (Goron Mines, Metalwork), element of Fire
      • Major city is Darunia in North Hyrule, element of Fire
      • Elder: Gor Larin (male, leader of the Goron Tribe, "Sage of Fire")
      • Major Characters: Dampeh, Dargor, Osgor, Galen
    • Yook/Yeti (Bigfoot-like people who live in the secluded mountains and ice fields of Snowpeak)
      • Culture based on the Russion elite culture
    • Anouki (Eskimo-like people who like in the lowlands and caverns of Snowpeak)
      • Culture based on the Russian proletariat culture with Inuit appearance
      • Capital is in the lowland of Snowpeak, element of Shadow
      • Elder: Chief Gonpho (male, "Sage of Shadow")
    • Subrosians (Shroaded lava people who live in Subrosia. The only remaining access is via the Path of Fire in the North)
      • Culture based on modern Arabic/Muslims
      • Represent Commerce (love Ore and use it to buy and sell things), element of Fire
    • Zuna (Race of shroaded Pyramid builders, only a few of which can be found)
      • Culture based on the ancient Egyptian culture
      • Major Characters: Sanday, Mosq

    CHARACTERS

    Some characters stay near their home, while others may wander around more or even visit other cities. Characters can be performing different actions depending on the period (time between each dungeon) the hero is currently in. There are 11 periods in all:

    PERIOD 1: Intro to first visit to Sewers (to get sword)

    PERIOD 2: Sword to Sacred Grove Temple

    PERIOD 3: Grove Temple to Abandoned Pyramid

    PERIOD 4: Abandoned Pyramid to second visit to Sewers (to access Library)

    PERIOD 5: Library to Mountaintop Mausoleum

    ----

    PERIOD 6: Mausoleum to third visit to sewers (after draining Lake Hylia, to get boomerang)

    PERIOD 7: Boomerang to Lakebed Lair

    PERIOD 8: Lakebed Lair to Snowpeak Caverns

    ----

    PERIOD 9: Snowpeak Caverns to last visit to sewers (to defeat boss, get page)

    PERIOD 10: Sewers to Tower of the Winds

    PERIOD 11: Tower of the Winds to Interloper Sanctum

    MAJOR CHARACTERS

    Crista: Link's childhood friend and possible love interest, potion maker in Ordon Shop, lover of nature.
    P1: Standing inside shop selling potions.
    P2: Quest: Wanders off to the woods to collect potion supplies, Julita asks you to go find her which leads you to the location of the Ordon Sword.
    P3: Standing inside shop selling potions. Mentions the existance of a rare mushroom for making potions.
    P4: At her house. Mentions the existance of a strange forest in the far east which is rumored to contain exotic vegetation, but she hasn't been able to find it. [Lost Woods]
    P5: At potion shop. Tells Link about rumors she's heard concerning flowers which blow up when you touch them - talk about weird plants!
    - Sidequests: Multiple fetch quests throughout the game to collect area-specific vegetation to make new potions.
    P6: Mentions the existance of a very knowledgable lumberjack who lives in the forest just north of town. Just the other day he was rambling on about treasure in the lake?

    Tristan: Link's caregiver and respected member of the Ordon community
    P1: Quest: Tristan organizes the torch lighting race for the festival, the prize for which is the Ordon Shield.
    P2-3: Walking around the northeast part of town. Talks about the dangers of the areas near the village, but he's also heard of treasure nearby.
    P4-5: At his house. Mentions to Link about the shops in Hyrule Castle Town and Kakariko City and all the cool stuff he could find!
    P6: Walking around the south part of town. Mentions to Link about the lumberjack and how he was just talking about Lake Hylia and the sewer grates.

    Ordona: Guardian Light Spirit of the Ordon region, and Link's guide on his journey. She resides in Link's lantern.
    P1: Upon completion of the torch race, Ordona speaks to Link and introduces herself
    P2: After getting back to town after getting the sword, Ordona directs Link to the woods in the west where she detects evil
    P4: After hearing about the Dark Tribe in the Ancient Library, Ordona suggests Link speak to the Council of Elders
    P5: After escaping the Castle, if Link doesn't talk to an NPC to tell him, she suggests he visit the northern mountains next.
    P6: If haven't talked to the lumberjack or gone back to the sewer to drain Lake Hylia, Ordona will appear if you venture beyond Ordon, Kakariko or Castle Town. She will direct you to the sewer entrance that gets you to the Lake Hylia section.

    Rudy: Blacksmith for the Ordon region, and old friend of Tristan
    P1: During the festival, Rudy does smithing demonstrations and bestows the Ordon Shield to Link when he wins the race
    P2-3: Sidequest: When Link finds Rudy's cave west of town, he asks Link to bring him some water to fill his tank
    P4-7: Rudy is available to upgrade Link's sword, but it will require Metal Ore (found in Subrosia, can access anytime after Mountaintop Mausoleum)
    P8-10: Still available to upgrade if needed. Also talks about the old stories of the Master Sword.

    Carock: Wizard and member of the Dark Tribe, who you fight in the Eastern Ruins and possibly elsewhere.

    Zirna: Enchantress and member of the Dark Tribe who is responsible for bringing most of the bosses to life.

    Belahim: Ghirahim-like Dark Tribe leader who you fight at the end of the game in the Dark Palace.

    Ulo: Mayor of Ordon and Council member
    P1: Walking around Ordon Village. Bestows his old lantern onto Link so he can compete in the festival torch race.
    P2-5: In the Chamber of Elder in Hyrule Castle. Asks Link about how things are going back home and rambles on about his daily grind.
    P6: Tells Link all about the underground sewer system, and the ancient levers which allowed various waterways to be drained. He thinks the Lake Hylia one might even still be functional!

    Etnaya: Proprieter of the Amphitheater at Kakariko.

    GREAT FAIRIES

    Great Fairies, who are creatures of light, are especially sensitive to the darkness that's spreading across Hyrule and require a large number of artifacts attributed to them to cleanse their magic and help link in his quest...

    Great Fairy of Courage (Faron Woods) - Need to collect 99 Mystic Jade and she upgrades your quiver

    Great Fairy of Power (Death Mountain) - Need to collect 99 Goron Amber and she upgrades your bomb bag

    Great Fairy of Wisdom (Snowpeak) - Need to collect 99 Alchemy Stone and she upgrades your magic meter (half)

    Great Fairy of Kindness (Lake Hylia) - Need to collect 99 Goddess Plume and she upgrades your boomerang

    Great Fairy of Magic (North Hyrule) - Need to collect 99 Magic Crystal and she upgrades your bracelet

    Great Fiary of Understanding (North Hyrule) - Need to collect 99 Subrosian Ore and she upgrades your sword


    DUNGEONS / MAJOR AREAS

    • Eastern Ruins/Hyrulian Sewers (Ongoing Temple) - Earth/Shadow/Water themes
      • Item: Ordon Sword (Faron Woods Sewer access, G12)
      • Key 1 (Faron Woods Sewer access, G12, after getting Bow)
      • Boss: Carock, Dark Tribe (Eastern Ruins, after obtaining key) - get heart piece, warp leads to Old Kasuto Town. "You may have defeated me, but you're too late to stop what my people have already set in motion!"
      • Key 2 (Eastern Ruins Sewer access, G9, after getting Bracelet)
      • Big Key (Lake Hylia Sewer access, E10, after draining water)
      • Item: Boomerang (Eastern Ruins, after obtaining big key)
      • Boss Key (Sewer access from within Ruins)
      • Boss: Big Poe and Poes - get heart piece
      • Reward: Book of Mudora page
      • Major Enemies: Tentacles
      • Notes: Temple is on-going through the first 1/4-1/3 of the game and requires you find multiple access in the overworld to progress in the temple. Puzzles mostly focus on water level in the lower sewers and defeating enemies and moving things in the upper ruins.
    • Sacred Grove - Sacred Grove Temple (2nd) - Forest/Earth themes
      • Miniboss: Mothulita swarm
      • Item: Bow
      • Boss: Gohma
      • Reward: Book of Mudora page
      • Major Enemies: Green ChuChus, Tentacles, Ropes, Keese, Green Tektites
      • Notes: Most straightforward temple with a dynamic similar to LoZ dungeons. Minor puzzles dealing with torches and enemies, and some inside-outside trekking.
    • Desert Expanse - Abandoned Pyramid (3rd) - Desert/Metal/Shadow themes (remnants of Lanayru Mining Facility)
      • Goal: Collect all of the parts needed to build the Gerudo Airship, take them back to the Gerudo who repair their ship and leave the beach. When you find them in their new home in the desert you have to chase them down to get the Boss Key.
      • Miniboss: Lanmola
      • Item: Bracelet
      • Boss: Manhandla
      • Reward: Book of Mudora page
      • Notes: Pyramid theme lends to there being Gibdos EVERWHERE! Also remnants of the mining facility means a lot of conveyor belts and plenty of boxes to move.
    • Death Mountain - Mountaintop Mausoleum (4th) - Fire/Shadow themes
      • Miniboss: Stone Arrghus
      • Miniboss: Dodongos (will appear in a few rooms AFTER the boss has been defeated and the mausoleum illuminated)
      • Item: Pegasus Boots
      • Boss: Vire Sorceror
      • Reward: Book of Mudora page
      • Notes: Combination of shadow and fire themes has never been done before! Maybe have a system of "collecting" or freeing Goron spirits as a way to power your lantern and explore deeper into the mausoleum. Upon the destruction of the ultimate spirit, Mogura, the entire mausoleum is illuminated.
    • Lake Hylia - Lakebed Lair (5th) - Water/Forest themes
      • Miniboss: Aquamentus
      • Miniboss: Plasmarine
      • Item: Hookshot
      • Boss: Great Lizard Gleeok
      • Reward: Book of Mudora page
      • Notes: Since the Lake has been drained, this is a very non-traditional water dungeon. Only one or two (shallow) water rooms in the beginning, several rooms consist of mud and muck which act like quicksand (combination of running and pegasus boots and hookshotting past pits will be used). Later rooms have more water dynamic so some water can be returned to lake after temple is complete!
    • Snowpeak - Snowpeak Caverns (6th) - Ice/Spirit themes
      • Miniboss: Stalfos
      • Miniboss: Ice Keese Swarm
      • Item: Cane
      • Boss: Frigid Fiend Kholdstare
      • Reward: Book of Mudora (unenchanted)
      • Notes:
    • Septen Heights - Tower of the Winds (7th) - Wind/Seasonal themes
      • Minibosses: Ice Keese Swarm, Fire Keese Swarm, Grim Creeper
      • Item: Light Shield
      • Boss: Helmaroc/Kargaroc
      • Reward: Book of Mudora (enchanted, complete)
      • Notes: Each level of the tower houses a different type of weather/wind. Puzzles in each level will depend on the type of weather and may depend on elemental items (lamp for fire, cane for ice, etc). Light shield used heavily (redirect ice blasts/beams, use light beams to make plants grow, absorb heat and send it out at ice, block wind gusts)
      • NB: The last two unique combinations not used in this tower are saved for the last dungeon (Electric/Earth - Light Storms, Electric - Heavy Storms)
        • Floor 1 (NW): Light Breezes (Earth)
        • Floor 2 (W): Cool Breezes (Water, Mini-boss: Ice Keese Swarm)
        • Floor 3 (SW): Warm Breezes (Fire)
        • Floor 4 (S): Warm Rains (Fire/Earth, Mini-boss: Fire Keese Swarm)
        • Floor 5 (SE): Light Rains (Water/Fire)
        • Floor 6 (E): Warm Storms (Fire/Electric, Mini-Boss: Grim Creeper)
        • Floor 7 (NE): Cold Rains (Water/Earth)
        • Floor 8 (N): Cold Storms (Water/Electric, Boss: Halmaroc)
    • Forest of Light/Lost Woods - Interloper Sanctum (8th) - Shadow theme
      • Miniboss: Dark Link
      • Item: Light Arrows
      • Boss: Belahim (Dark Tribe Leader)
      • Notes: Large two-story palace. The top (entry) story will be almost completely dark, with very few lamps, so you must proceed almost blind. Few puzzles, but lots of enemies! The nearly last room will house a mini-boss fight with Dark Link. The bottom story will play like a game of Pente/Othello/Go, where each room can be switched to either light or dark by lamp-lighting puzzles, mirror shield reflection puzzles, Light Arrows, or some other dynamic. You win the game by lighting up five rooms in a row without Dark Link extinguishing them (playing board is the map), you can proceed to Belahim. As an added bonus, each room pays homage to various dungeons!

    Ordona Province: the southernmost are of South Hyrule which contains Ordon Village, and hides the Lantern (Ulo's House), a Bottle (Rudy sidequest), the Forged Sword (Blacksmith's Cave) and the Ordon Shield (Ordon Shop)

    Faron Woods: The dense woods west of Ordon Village which contain the Sacred Grove, and hide the Bow (Grove Temple) and an Odd Mushroom.

    Lost Woods (Forest of Light): the eastern forested area of Hyrule which has fallen into shadow recently, and now houses the Interloper Sanctum. Also hides the Deku Mask.

    Hyrule Field: Major open area and hub in the center of Hyrule. Swarming with Green Soldiers.

    Southern Beach:

    Lake Hylia: Large lake and home of the Lakeside Laboratory. Swarming with Blue ChuChus.

    Faron Province: The southern and central expanse of Hyrule which consists of LonLon Ranch, Hyrule Castle Town, Kakariko Village and the Eastern Ruins.

    Lon Lon Ranch:

    Hyrule Castle Town is one of the largest cities in Hyrule. The following quest items can be found here: Royal Shield (shop), Bottle (shop), Ocarina of Wind (Relic Collector's house)

    Kakariko City: The new cultural hub of Hyrule and home to the Amphitheater, Bomb Shop, and a major cemetary.

    Eldin Province: The northern and eastern expanse of Hyrule which consists of Death Mountain, Goron City and Desert Expanse

    Goron City: Technologically budding city and home to the Goron people. You can find the Mountaintop Mausoleum and the White Sword (Blacksmith) and Goron Hammer (Mausoleum) here.

    Death Mountain: Dangerous area swarming with Red Knight, ChuChus and Tektites and active with large steam geysers.

    Subrosia

    Desert Expanse

    Lanayru Province: The Northern and western expanse of Hyrule which consists of Zora's Domain, Septen Heights and Snowpeak.

    Septen Heights: Town of rope bridges and cave dwellings, home of a newly evolved Rito people (on this timeline). Location of the Tower of the Winds where the Mirror Shield can be found.

    Zora's Domain: Find the Zora's Flippers here, but beware of the unfriendly Zora people.

    Snowpeak: Home of the Anouki and Yook (Yeti) people and location of the Snowpeak Caverns.

    Hidden Village: Home of Impa the Elder. Rumors state it was once a thriving Sheikah city.

    Northern Hyrule: Northern expanse of Hyrule is divided into Eastern and Western halves. Many of these cities are just beginning to be established.

    Rauru Town: Settlement of Hylians who broke off from Hyrule proper due to disagreement on governing style and direction. They establish the Hyli Province and reintroduce the Religion of Hylia Her Grace to the north.

    North Castle: Rauru Town's fortified base and head of government for the Hylians of the North.

    Tantari Desert: Home of a cave which houses a large Goriya, and a small settlement of Zuna. Find a bottle hidden here.

    Ruto Town: Mountainous city, populated by a few humans and a band of Zolas who live mostly in the Northern Waters. Home of a spell-teaching wise man and the man known as Error. Get a Book of Spells here from a wise man.

    Saria Town: Water town just north of Death Mountain. The River Man will lover the bridge over the river, allowing easy transit between locations. Hunt down the witch to obtain the Moon Pearl.

    Moruge Swamp: Swampy peninsula south of North Castle which houses a single hut and plenty of danger. Find a shovel here.

    King's Tomb: Landmark with a tunnel just south that leads to the Island Palace. Graves marked include the King Nohansen (of Hyrule), King Mutoh (of Cobble), Brant, Bremeur, Doylan and Max (Cobble Knights).

    Mido Town: Harbor town which connects Eastern and Western Hyrule.

    Parapa Desert: Location of Parapa Palace and the hidden underground civilization of the Garo.

    Island Palace

    Darunia Town: A network of caves in which people live and home of the traveling Goron Marketplace.

    Nabooru Town: Oasis paradise in the middle of a desert and mountain expance which offers two large fountains among its houses. Home to a rogue (peaceful) Gerudo faction that broke off from the Gerudo Sky Pirates.

    Valley of Death/Path of Fire: Small cemetary south of Nabooru Town that contains lava flows which connect to Subrosia.

    Kasuto Town: Small settlement which houses the Ancient Library.

    Three Eye Rock: Located at the southeastern Seashore and home of the Great Palace, now mostly in ruins.

    Maze Island: Composed of a labyrinthine network of roads and houses a hidden grotto (with a bottle).
